notice.                                                                                                                                         · Protection from Unfair Eviction: A tenant cannot be evicted without a valid reason (such as non-payment) and must be given a reasonable opportunity to contest the eviction through a Controller.

· Payment Deadline: Landlords have the right to receive rent by the 10th day of every month, unless a different date is specifically mentioned in the contract.
· Recovery of Arrears: If a tenant fails to pay, the landlord has the legal right to sue for the recovery of rent arrears through the Rent Controller.
· Utility Reimbursement: Landlords have the right to demand timely payment for utilities (electricity, water, gas) if they are not included in the base rent, to avoid accumulating debt in the landlord's name.
· Prevention of Waste: Landlords have the right to ensure the tenant does not cause "material
deterioration" or permanent damage to the property.
· Right of Entry for Inspection: After giving reasonable prior notice, a landlord has the right to enter the premises to inspect the condition of the property or carry out necessary repairs.

consent of the landlord.
